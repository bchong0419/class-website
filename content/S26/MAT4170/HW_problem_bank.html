Exam questions

  1. Quotienting free group.
  2. On HW X, Exercise 3, we proved that there were (at least) five non-isomorphic groups. Prove that the Quaternion group is not either.
  3. [4] The set $\mathbb{R} \cup \{\infty\}$ with the operations $x \oplus y = \min(x, y)$ and $x \otimes y = x + y$ is called the min tropical semiring, and it is not a ring—but it's close! Check which properties of a ring it has and which it fails to have.
    1. [4] Let $G$ be a group that acts on a set $X$. We say that $a,b \in X$ are in the same orbit if there exists some $g \in G$ such that $g \cdot a = b$. Prove that "is in the same orbit as" is an equivalence relation, by showing it is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.
    2. [3] If $G$ acts on $X$, the group action is called transitive if for each $x, x' \in X$ there exists some $g \in G$ such that $g \cdot x = x'$.
      1. Is the "usual" action of $D_4$ on the directions $\{E, N, W, S\}$ transitive?
      2. Is the "usual" action of $S_5$ on the set $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$ transitive?
      3. Give an example of a group action which is faithful but not transitive.
    3. [4] We call a group action free when $g \cdot x \neq x$ for all $g \neq e$.
      1. Is the usual action of $S_5$ on the set $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$ free?
      2. Give an example of a set $X$ a group action of $D_4$ on $X$ which is faithful but not free.
      3. Prove that every free group action is faithful.
      4. Give an example of a group and a group action which is both transitive and free.
  5. We say that a commutative ring $(R, +, \times)$ (which is not the trivial ring) is called an integral domain if whenever $a, b \in R \setminus \{0_R\}$, then $a \times b \neq 0_R$.
    1. Let $R_1 = \mathbb{Z}_4$. Give an example of $a, b \in R_1$ that shows that $R_1$ is not an integral domain.
    2. Let $R_2 = \mathbb{R}^{2 \times 2}$ be the set of $2 \times 2$ matrices with real valued entries. Give an example of $a, b \in R_2$ that shows that $R_2$ is not an integral domain.
    3. Let $R_3$ be a field such as $\mathbb{Q}$ or $\mathbb{Z}_{17}$. Prove that $R_3$ is an integral domain.
    4. Let $R_4 = \mathbb{Z}$. Tell me what you know about the integers that allows you to conclude that $R_4$ is an integral domain.
    5. Let $R_5 = \mathbb{C}[C_3]$, the group ring of $C_3 = \{e, r, r^2\}$ with complex coefficients. Let $x = e - r$, and let $y = e + r + r^2$. Multiply together $x \times y$. Is $R_5$ an integral domain?
