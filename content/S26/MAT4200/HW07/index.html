Exercise 3 [7] (Curvature)

Let \( M \) be the surface given by the parametric equation \[ \mathbf{r}(u,v) = \begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \\ uv\end{pmatrix},\quad (u,v) \in (-1,1) \times (-1, 1), \] and let \(\mathbf{N} \colon M \to S^2\) be the Gauss map, where \[ S^2 = \{(x,y,z) \mid x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1\} \] denotes the unit sphere.

We will be calculating the various notions of curvature at \( p = \mathbf{r}(0,0) = (0,0,0). \)

  1. We begin by computing the Gauss map at each point and its differential, which we denote \( dN_p \colon T_pM \to T_{N(p)}(S^2) \), and which is defined at each point $p \in M$.
    1. Begin by computing $N_p \colon M \to S_2$ at each point $p = \mathbf{r}(u,v) \in M$ in terms of $u$ and $v$.
    2. Next, explain why the tangent space $T_pM$ at each point $p$ is identical to the tangent space \(T_{N(p)}(S^2)\) by comparing the two tangent planes geometrically.
    3. Compute the differential (Jacobian matrix) \( dN_p \colon T_pM \to T_{N(p)}(S^2) \) with respect to $u$ and $v$, and evaluate it at \( (u,v) = (0,0) \).
  2. Next, you will guess at what this map looks like:
    1. Use a computer to plot \(M\) around the point \( p = \mathbf{r}(0,0) = (0,0,0) \).
    2. Compute \( \mathbf{r}_u(0,0) \) and \( \mathbf{r}_v(0,0) \).
    3. Describe in words what \( dN_p(\mathbf{r}_u(0,0)) \) and \( dN_p(\mathbf{r}_v(0,0)) \) represent.
    4. By looking at the plot, make a guess of what you expect to get after you compute \( dN_p(\mathbf{r}_u(0,0)) \) and \( dN_p(\mathbf{r}_v(0,0)) \).
    5. Now compute these values. If your guess was wrong, figure out why, and explain where your intuition went wrong.
  3. Compute the eigenvalues of \(dN_p\) at \( p = \mathbf{r}(0,0) = (0,0,0) \) and their corresponding eigenvectors. Prove that the eigenvectors are orthogonal to each other. (The (unit) eigenvectors are called the principal directions and the (negative) eigenvalues are called the principal curvatures at \( p \).)
  4. The mean curvature is the mean of the principal curvatures. Compute it.
  5. The Gaussian curvature is the product of the principal curvatures which is the determinant of \(dN_p\). Compute the Gaussian curvature.
  6. We say that a point $p$ on a surface is
    1. planar if \(dN_p\) is the zero matrix,
    2. hyperbolic if \( \det(dN_p) < 0\),
    3. parabolic if \( \det(dN_p) = 0\) but \(dN_p\) is nonzero, and
    4. elliptic if \( \det(dN_p) > 0\).
    How can we characterize the point \( p \) on \( M \) according to this classification?
  7. Draw or plot a smooth surface \( S \) where some points are elliptic, some are parabolic, and some are hyperbolic. Include the image here.
